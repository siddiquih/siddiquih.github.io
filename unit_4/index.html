Welcome to my personal blog! Ever wonder what experiences you may come across on a safari in Africa? It is definitely a once-in-a-lifeteime experience I would encourage everyone to see! On this site, you will find an about section, pictures from my travels, as well as tips on what to bring along, in addition to how to say safe when visiting a safari.

Here below, you will find pictures I have taken from my trips, as well as a short discription

This is a picture of a giraffe left by a film crew in Kenya. This animal is very tall. An intersting fact about the island in which the animals reside is that they overpopulated due to a lack of natural predators.

>

This picture of wild baboons was captured in Uganda. Normally, they are known to be carnivores; however, here they are known to eat vegetation and are occasionally fed by the local residents.

In this picture, taken in Nairobi, you will see buffalo in the background. I was cautioned to keep my distance, as they can be very dangerous when approached too closely.

This image was taken of a bull elephant crossing a rural country side. The bull elephant was leading its heard to eat vegetation in the grass fields. Our driver cautioned us to remain calm and distant from the animals, as any degree of sound or commotion would provoke the animals to attack, as they particularly do not like humans due to a history of poaching for ivory.>
